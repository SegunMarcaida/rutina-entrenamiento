# Rutina decisions

## 2026-06-30 — Pretemporada performance indoor

Decision: shift the plan toward performance for football preseason.

Meaning:
- Monday is a flexible running selector: football fartlek, pasadas, 5k, or 10k, with football fartlek as the default transfer option and the rule that Tuesday must stay viable.
- Tuesday is strength-performance: mobility, back squat full ROM for 3 effective sets, tibialis between squat sets, then Nordics + hip flexor work, ATG split squat + weighted Cossack squat, and back extensions + obliques in the same machine.
- Wednesday is the highest-priority preseason session: indoor power plus mixed VO2 using one machine station, preferably bike or rower, with hang power cleans early while fresh.
- Thursday is upper-body strength plus shoulder and core: weighted pull-ups + weighted dips 5x5 superset, push press, two rounds of press + row, lateral raises, and strong core with ab wheel.
- Friday is rest/recovery or catch-up if a gym day was missed.
- Saturday and Sunday are flexible sport days with load rules.

Rejected:
- Wall drives and band starts, because they are awkward and not preferred.
- VO2 circuits that require moving around the gym or occupying many stations.
- Pairing Nordics with back extensions, because back extensions pair better with obliques on the same machine.

## 2026-06-02 — Training model

Decision: use ALTIS + EXOS as the main model, with ATG/Knees Over Toes as the injury-resilience layer.

Meaning:
- Tuesday and Wednesday prioritize speed, acceleration, power, and athletic transfer for football 11.
- ATG-style work is included as targeted prehab, not as the dominant identity of the plan.
- Nordic curls, reverse Nordic curls, back extensions, QL extensions, tibialis work, and knee-over-toe patterns are kept for hamstring, knee, ankle, and trunk resilience.
- Vernon Griffith is included as a mobility and movement-quality reference, especially for hip, thoracic spine, ankle, and active range work.

Rejected:
- ATG/KOT as the dominant plan, because it may bias too much toward tissue capacity and not enough toward speed.
- Fartlek/conditioning as the dominant plan, because the main objective is athleticism, acceleration, and injury resistance, not endurance first.

References:
- ALTIS acceleration model: https://altis.world/articles/acceleration-development-chidi-enyia/
- EXOS speed model: https://www.teamexos.com/education/on-site-speed/
- Soccer mixed speed-power training: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/physiology/articles/10.3389/fphys.2017.01034/full
- Nordic hamstring injury prevention: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27752982/
- ATG principles: https://www.atgonlinecoaching.com/articles/the-10-atg-principles
- Vernon Griffith mobility reference: https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Vernon+Griffith+mobility

## 2026-06-02 — Tuesday and Wednesday split

Decision: use Tuesday for short acceleration and power, and Wednesday for speed/power plus injury-resilience work.

Meaning:
- Tuesday targets 0-10 m acceleration qualities: first-step power, horizontal force, jump lunges, bounds, and gym-compatible explosive work.
- Wednesday keeps a speed and athleticism objective, but must include an indoor fallback because 10-30 m sprints may not fit inside the gym.
- Wednesday also carries the main resilience pieces: Nordic curls, reverse Nordic curls, back extensions, and QL extensions.

Open constraint:
- The available gym space for 10-30 m sprint work is uncertain.

Decision: use an indoor speed-intent fallback when 10-30 m is not available.

Meaning:
- Primary indoor fallback: wall drills, band/cable marches, low box jumps, short bounds, pogo hops, lateral skater bounds, and other short-space drills.
- Optional outdoor fallback: if weather and surface are good, use short asphalt accelerations outside.
- Avoid making the plan depend on long sprint space inside the gym.
